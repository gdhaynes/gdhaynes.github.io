What is a 15 Minute City

For all of human history nothing has impacted the way we live more than out ability to move around in the environment. For nearly all of human history human mobility consisted of a few options, travel on foot, pack animal, and human and wind powered water craft. In cities this resulted in a very dense development pattern, simply because it made traveling from a to b easier if the distance was short. This pattern persisted for thousands of years until the industrial revolution. The industrial revolution brought with it railways and the first mass public transport. These systems allowed development to de densify to a small degree. Then came the automobile, and with it came mass de densifying of cities all over the world, with North America leading the way. The unfortunate result has been car dependance for much of North America, which comes with a host of problems including pollution, excessive resource consumption, poor land use, and exacerbation of wealth inequality. In order to address these issue urban planners have suggested a concept to address these issues called the 15 minute city.

What is a 15 minute city

The idea behind the concept of a 15 minute city is to build or retrofit cities in such a way the majority of daily necessities and services are located within a 15-minute walk or bike ride of where people live. Carlos Moreno, urbanist and professor at the Sorbonne University in Paris first came up with the idea in 2016. He wanted everyone to have easy access to shops, schools, doctors, gyms, parks, restaurants and cultural institutions without the need of a car.

Defining Characteristics

  • Emphasis on biking and walking

    Generally the pattern of development in North America has been centering design of cities and neighborhoods around cars, but the 15 minute city is centered around bike and pedestrian transportation.

  • Easy access to amenities

    Most car centric cities have poor access to amenities like grocery stores, banks, other stores, restaurants, and parks. The 15 minute city by design has easy access to all amenities

  • Human scale buildings

    Most car centric cities have poor urban form, this is a result of both car centric transportation and poor planning policy. Buildings are either low density single family housing or ultra high density high rises. The 15 minute city focuses on diverse housing and buildings that are generally no more than a few stories tall.

Advantages

The 15 minute city is advantageous over car centric cities for a number of reasons. First is that their denser housing means higher revenues for the city. Second is improved air quality from lack. Third is increased health of residents due to a focus on walking and biking. Fourth is more inviting city spaces such as parks, plazas, and squares. Lastly is less environmental impact.

Criticisms

The only real disadvantage of the 15 minute city is that it is hard to implement in areas with a focus on car centric development. This is because getting out of the 15 minute city to a neighboring community is difficult if there are no transit connections
