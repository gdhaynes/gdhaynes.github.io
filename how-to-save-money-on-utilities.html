How to Save Money on Utilities

Energy prices are on the rise, fortunately we can do a few things in our homes to save energy, and thus save money on our bills.

  • Replace old light bulbs with LEDs

    Fortunately the price of LED light bulbs has dropped dramatically over the past few years making installing LED light bulbs a simple and inexpensive upgrade to any home that will help save you money on your electricity bills.

  • Use a programmable thermostat

    A programmable thermostat can be set to change the temperature setting of your home when you are away, they can be set to a higher temperature setting in summer and a lower temperature setting in the winter. By changing the temperature setting throughout the day your HVAC system wont have to work as much and will consume less energy.

  • Keep your HVAC filters, vents, and radiators clean

    If you have a forced air HVAC system, make sure to regularly change your filters, you can find the filter change interval in your systems manual or by documentation on the replacement filter. Check out our article on how to change an HVAC filter Here. In addition, keep your vents clean and outdoor HVAC unit clear of debris and plant growth. If you have radiators, keeping them free of dust will allow them to better conduct heat and save you money.

  • Turn down the heat on your hot water heater

    Many hot water heaters are set to 140 fahrenheit, 120 will work fine for most people.

  • Make use of fans

    Fans, especially ceiling fans are great for circulating air in a room. Ceiling fans can help pull warm air down in the winter, and bring cool air up in the summer thus helping you save. If you don't have ceiling fans, or don't have the means to have them installed a pedestal fan also works well.

  • Unplug unused devices

    Some appliances like fridges need to stay plugged in all the time, others like tvs, computers, and other electronics will often draw power even when they are off, use a power strip on these devices and turn them off when they are not in use.

  • Use window treatments

    Using window treatments, like curtains, and blinds on hot sunny days will help limit the amount the sunlight that enters and warms your home.

  • Seal Ductwork

    If your home has exposed ductwork check to see if the joints have been sealed, this is evidenced by shiny foil tape or mastic on the joints. Sealed ductwork means that the conditioned air only goes where it's intended to go. Check out our article on how to seal ductwork Here.

  • Add insulation

    It can be difficult to add insulation in an existing home, however there are a few spaces that usually lend themselves to added insulation including attics, basements, ductwork, and hot water pipes. Adding insulation can be a hard and dangerous job, be sure to consult a pro if you have doubts or questions.

  • Install energy efficient appliances

    When an appliance fails, or you are renovating, replace old energy hungry appliances with more efficient ones. These can be easily identified by the energy star logo.

  • Upgrade windows and doors

    When windows and doors age they begin leaking conditioned air to the outside. this can be rectified by adding or fixing weatherstripping. In extreme case replacing the windows and doors may be in order.

  • Use low flow fixtures

    Low flow showers and faucets will help reduce your water bill, as well as your hot water heating bill.

  • Use your dishwasher

    If you have a dishwasher, use it. Dishwashers use less water and energy than washing by hand. Also make sure to wash a full load.

  • Run appliances at night

    If your utility offers off peak pricing, that is lower energy prices at later hours of the day, use those hours to run your major appliances.

  • Make sure windows and door are closed properly

    It can be common for a window or door to stick open letting conditioned air outside. Go around your home and make sure all your windows and doors are closed tightly when the heat or air conditioning is on. Similarly be sure storm windows and doors are closed as well especially in winter when cold winter winds can rob your home of energy.
