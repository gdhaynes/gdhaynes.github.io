International Builders Show 2023 Trip Report

It was sometime in late 2022 when DB pitched the idea of going to the National Association of Homebuilders (IBS) in Las Vegas Nevada. Personally I don't see the appeal of Vegas, but DB and I both worked very hard over the past two years, and the company has done well, and we had never been to a builders show so I figured why not. Plane tickets were bought, a hotel was booked, and registration was paid, and soon enough I found myself on an airplane headed to sin city.

Recap

This was our first time at IBS and the Las Vegas Convention Center, both of us were completely blown away at the size of of it, at 2.5 million square feet its one of the largest convention centers in the world. We were pleasantly surprised to find that in addition to IBS there was also the Kitchen and Bath Industry Show (KBIS) and National Hardware Show (NHS) all happening at the same time, together all the expos took place during Design and Construction week and occupied nearly every square foot of the convention center. In total there were ~110,000 people in attendance and thousands of vendors. Over the next three days we walked around 30 miles just inside the convention halls, through IBS, KBIS, and NHS, we attended a few demos and seminars but spent most of our time roaming through the halls looking for the latest in tools and building technology.

Our first day was spend mostly looking through KBIS which occupied two convention halls. There were strong showings from lesser known and international appliance manufacturers, in particular there were a lot of ranges, vent hoods, tubs, showers, faucets, shower drains, and almost every sink design imaginable. There were also lots of demonstration kitchens. We did see a little bit of IBS on that day and saw some electrical and HVAC vendors.

Our second day was spent mostly at IBS, which was spread through two and a half convention halls, there were strong showings from window and door manufacturers, building material suppliers, and HVAC suppliers. We were disappointed that there weren't many tool manufacturers. We also briefly checked out the NHS which consisted of mostly asian manufacturers presumably looking for distributer and vendors here in the United States.

Our third day was spent mostly at IBS, we checked out the outdoor exhibits and were hoping to see Timbertech, whose products we have installed on a number of decks and porches, however they were not there. There were some complete prefab homes and accessory dwelling units, we were generally impressed with their look and apparent quality. We also attended a seminar on client contractor relations, which we found to be mostly helpful. In addition we attended a roofing demo where they demoed how to install over Zip system roof decking. Which we learned that the primary advantage of Zip Sytem is that it makes the building water tight before the roofing and cladding are installed. This allows flexibility with scheduling other trades like siding installers, roofers, electricians, plumbers, etc.

Overall we enjoyed Design and Construction week and will likely attend in the future. We'll definitely do some more planning and try to identify speakers, and demos that we want to go to before hand

What we liked

    Warmboard radiant system

    This is a radiant heat system that uses a standard wood core, but features a continuous aluminum cladding at the top, this gives great thermal conductivity and increases the efficiency of the system.

    More Information

    Naturekast cabinets

    This is a manufacturer of outdoor cabinets, and kitchens. Their products have a nice look and appear very sturdy.

    More Information

    Rheem heat pump hot water heater

    This is a hot water heater that uses a heat pump to heat the water, Rheem claimed these units as the most efficient hot water heaters on the market.

    More Information

    Karran Laminate undermount sink

    Undermount sinks look great for their lack of edge, but mounting them to laminate poses a problem. Because laminate has an mdf backer it will swell when water contacts it. But Karran had a demo installation where the undermount sink mounts directly to the laminate creating a water tight seal.

    More Information

    POMCube whole house battery backups

    We've all heard of generator backups, but POMCube makes a whole house battery backup, we like this because it is less maintenance than a conventional backup generator.

    More Information

    Huber products

    Huber makes a line of innovative construction products, including Zip System which is wall and roof material that has an integrated water resistant barrier (WRB). They also make a tape that seals the joints between panels. We've used this product before and love it because it saves time and money compared to a conventional system. New to us is Exacor, a magnesium oxide underlayment panel that is used in applications where sound deadening and fire protection are needed.

    More Information

    RSG Panels

    RSG makes a panel product that consists of a heavy wire structure with a foam core. These panels can easily be assembled onsite, into a building shell. Once this is done mechanicals are roughed in and the entire building shot with concrete. This creates a strong and energy efficient structure that goes up fast.

    More Information
